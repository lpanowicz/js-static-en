  • Biography
Acclaimed by the leading Polish musical magazine Ruch Muzyczny as “always excellent in contemporary piano repertoire”, Julia Samojło has performed as a soloist and with many prominent artists across Europe and Israel in venues like The Concertgebouw, The Barbican Hall, The Wigmore Hall, The Rudolfinum, The Tel Aviv Museum of Art, The Warsaw Philharmonic, Witold Lutoslawski Concert Studio of The Polish Radio. She has appeared in concerts with The Sinfonia Varsovia, The Polish Radio Symphony Orchestra, The Poznań Philharmonics and was invited by The Polish Radio 2 to record Stefan Kisielewski’s Piano Concerto with The Polish National Radio Symphony Orchestra of Katowice. Her performance of Wojciech Kilar’s Piano Concerto during The International Music Festival Radio Autumn in Prague, was described by Hudebni Rozhledy as “handed brilliantly”. She has appeared in concerts at The International Festival of Contemporary Music Warsaw Autumn, Pre- LSO recital in The Barbican Hall, Kunst10Daagse in Bergen, Holland, From Chopin to Górecki- Sources and Inspirations Festival in Warsaw, World Percussion Congress in Jelenia Góra, Young Songmakers Almanac directed by Graham Johnson in London and others. Julia’s repertoire ranges from Bach to 21st century world premieres.  Her passionate performances  have been acknowledged by many distinguished composers of all generations and critics. She has enjoyed many years of collaboration with The Polish Composers Union- more than 30 of her live performances including numerous premieres have been recorded for their library and aired on The Polish Radio 2. The world premiere of Benedykt Konowalski’s VIII Symphony concertante for piano, percussion and string orchestra at The Warsaw Musical Meetings Festival  was released by Musica Sacra. In 2013 Julia will record an album featuring 12 Etudes, Piano Quintet and Piano Trio by Krzysztof Baculewski to be released by DUX. As a natural collaborator, she has deeply enjoyed working with with violinist Krzysztof Jakowicz, cellists Karolina Jaroszewska and Tomasz Strahl, percussionist Leszek Lorent , clarinetist Artur Pachlewski, saxophonist Pawel Gusnar, singers Anna Mikołajczyk- Niewiedział, Anna Radziejewska, Szymon Komasa,  conductors Jacek Kaspszyk, Łukasz Borowicz, Marek Moś, Krzesimir Dębski.  Her deep interest in chamber music is also expressed in her doctorate thesis that examines collaboration in percussion- piano duo focusing on works by Kazimierz Serocki, Maurice Ohana and Harrison Birtwistle. Julia has received numerous prizes and artistic scholarships including:  I prize at the International Competition for Young Performers in Athens, 1st prize at the competiton for the leading performance of a specially commissioned work by an Israeli composer in the framework of Tel Hai International Piano Master Classes, the scholarships of The Prime Minister of Poland, The President of The City of Szczecin, The President of The City of Bydgoszcz, The Minister of Culture and National Heritage, grants from Adam Mickiewicz Institute. She is a graduate of The Feliks Nowowiejski Academy of Music in Bydgoszcz where she studied with Tatiana Shebanova. She finished artistic internship in contemporary performance with Szabolcs Esztenyi and postgraduate and doctoral studies with Maja Nosowska at The Fryderyk Chopin University of Music in Warsaw. In 2010 she received a doctoral Erasmus scholarship to study at the Guildhall School of Music and Drama where she worked with Pamela Lidiard and received coachings from Graham Johnson. Julia participated in numerous master classes in New York, London, Bergen, Israel, Oxford, where she worked with such distinguished artists as Halina Czerny-Stefańska, Emanuel Krasovsky, Lee Kum-Sing, Jerome Rose, Alexander Kobrin, Alon Goldstein, John Perry,  Nelly Ben- Or, Graham Johnson, Julius Drake, Roger Vignoles, Martin Katz. Since September 2011 Julia has held a position of an Artist Fellow at The Guildhall School of Music and Drama in London under the artistic supervision of Ronan O’Hora where she has also taught piano and worked closely with the vocal and composition departments.  In the academic year of 2011- 2012 she was also a piano teacher at The City University in London.
